The equal sign and the data entry register

When you enter numbers in the calculator. While we enter the number, it goes into a data register. It is not moved into the formula before we do anything that tell that the data entry is complete. The formula area is displayed in this way, with a “_” after the number. When we press “=” the formula is calculated one final time, and moved down to line two in the formula area, included with the result. If you want to do anything more with it now, you have to push the “edit” button, before you write anything else. You can also select and copy the text, to a notes book, or something if you need it later.

When the formula is finalized and moved to the second line, it is never recalculated again. If you have used variables in your formula, you and you want to play with different values, you need to do that before you press the “=” sign. A line below the formula show what variable values it is calculated with. This is for documentation use, and to make you confident that you have the right answer. Before it is finalized, you have “=> <number> with <variables> on the second line. This is for cut & paste operations to take both formula, variables and answer to a document even if it is only an intermediate answer. If you change the variables, a new result appears immediately when you tab out of the variable field.
